WEB EXTENSION TO DETECT SINHALA YOUTUBE FAKES AND HATE SPEECH

YouTube is the world's largest user-generated video content provider, and it has developed into a significant platform for disseminating multimedia information. And YouTube has over 2.6 billion monthly active users. However, with such a vast audience, there is a risk of spreading false news and hate speech through the platform. Despite the company's efforts to combat this issue, false information and hate speech continue to proliferate on the site.

In recent years, social media has become a primary source of information, but it has also led to the proliferation of misinformation, deep fakes, and hate speech. Technology advancements have made it easy for individuals with limited technical knowledge to create deep fake content, which can be used for both positive purposes like filmmaking and VR, as well as for malicious intent such as inciting political unrest or staging terrorist activities. Hate speech has also surged on social media platforms, with major platforms struggling to combat violent content targeting various groups based on race, religion, gender, and more. The intricate nature of hate speech, influenced by linguistic nuances, poses a challenge. To address these issues, a YouTube video rating system with hate speech and deep fake detection, along with similarity checks for video content and metadata, is proposed as a valuable resource to help users make informed decisions about video content. This solution involves the use of digital approaches, including deep learning models, artificial intelligence, and convolutional neural networks, to ensure accuracy in detecting and addressing these problems.

To combat the spread of false information and hate speech, YouTube has implemented various measures such as machine learning algorithms and human moderators. However, these measures have not been fully implemented and the data is not continuous. Additionally, researching nonfiction texts in languages other than English can be difficult because the resources and tools needed for this task may not be readily available. Therefore, the primary objective of this research is to develop a system as a web extension that assesses the probability of false content including deep fake and hate speech occurring in Sinhala videos, thereby contributing to the maintenance of content quality and the promotion of a safer and more inclusive YouTube environment for Sinhala-speaking users.

Research Problem and Solution

  • Research Problem

    The growing use of social media in Sri Lanka has led to a number of societal challenges stemming primarily from the rampant spread of misinformation and hate speech on these platforms. This interconnected problem has profound implications, as false information not only misleads individuals, but also creates an environment of discrimination, stigmatization and even violence. Of particular concern is YouTube's role in fueling these issues, with some users using the platform to spread false narratives and incite violence through hate speech. Although YouTube has implemented measures such as community reporting and machine learning algorithms to limit such content, their accuracy remains inconsistent. Existing research on this matter focuses mainly on detecting hate speech and misinformation in English and other major languages, with limited attention to languages such as Sinhala. With the growing presence of Sinhala YouTubers and content, the need to effectively detect hate speech becomes paramount. Additionally, existing YouTube rating plugins that rely on metrics such as views and likes, fail to rate video content based on its potential for hate speech, creating a challenge in maintaining video quality and accuracy in this issue. Therefore, striking a balance between freedom of expression and responsible content moderation while addressing the specific linguistic nuances of Sinhala remains a pressing research issue.

  • Research Solution

    The web extension, aptly named "RateMyTube," has been thoughtfully crafted to function as a robust tool dedicated to the evaluation and categorization of YouTube videos. Its core functionality revolves around the assessment of video content and user interactions. One of its standout features is the provision of class names or categories for videos, a classification achieved through a meticulous analysis of similarity percentages computed by the extension. These similarity percentages are the result of a comprehensive comparison of various aspects of the video, including its title and description, with the actual content featured in the video itself.

Research Objectives

The primary goal of this research is to develop a web extension that assigns ratings to Sinhala YouTube videos, helping viewers make informed decisions about whether to watch them based on the content. These ratings will consider various significant factors, including the likelihood of encountering deep fakes or hate speech in the video, as well as the percentage of similarities between a video's title and description with its audio and visual content.

  • 01 Hate speech Detection

    Identifying hate speech in Sinhala YouTube videos is a sub objective of this study. This web extension will give ratings for each video based on the probability of having hate speech, letting viewers know whether the video is worthwhile watching To accomplish the objective of "Identifying hate speech in Sinhala YouTube videos," the following sub-objectives must be achieved.

  • 02Deepfake Detection

    The main objective of this study is to identify Sinhala deep fake videos on YouTube by analyzing facial expressions in the video. To achieve this objective, the study will pay close attention to various factors, including facial movements (such as mouth expressions and eye contact), lighting, and other details that may appear unusual or out of place. By examining these details, the study can identify videos that have been manipulated or altered using deep fake technology.

  • 03 Identifying Similarity Between Video Title and Visual Content

    Studying the patterns found in the texts, this model can determine how similar two sets of text are to one another. Inputs for this model will include the video content that has been translated and summarized, as well as the title and description of the video that can be found on YouTube.

  • 04 Identifying Similarity Between Video Title and Audio Content

    The titles of videos and the descriptions of those videos are two of the most important parts of a YouTube video. Before watching the full content, the majority of users would first obtain an understanding of the video by using those components. As a result, it is important that the information contained in the video's title and description be included in the video’s audio content itself.
